While the public rallied around removing Louis DeJoy, the real disassembly of USPS was happening behind closed doors—bipartisan, deliberate, and systemic. From backdoor deals with logistics contractors to quiet union negotiations, this report traces the web of complicity that gutted America's last functional institution.

The Bipartisan Blade

Despite loud opposition to DeJoy's tenure, few in Congress acted meaningfully. Funding withheld, audits delayed, performance metrics falsified—this was not incompetence. It was alignment.

Internal Whistleblowers Silenced

A pattern emerges: whistleblowers are sidelined or disciplined for raising internal concerns about delivery cuts, toxic managers, and undelivered election mail. Many were former military, relying on their USPS jobs for survival.
